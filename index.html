Rules


The #1 rule is don't be stupid.

By signing up to be in the game, you are agreeing to all of the following rules. Breaking any of these rules will result in elimination.


OBJECT OF THE GAME:

To assassinate as many of your targets as possible while not being eliminated. Last one standing wins.


HOURS OF THE GAME:

8:00 AM - 3:30 PM. Kills may only be made on HRS campus.


APPROPRIATE WEAPONS:

Sock grenades (balled up CLEAN socks), which may be thrown as projectiles
Spoons which MAY NOT be thrown as projectiles

A hit with either of these weapons signifies death. Attempted kills with alternative weapons do not count. No NERF guns or water guns. No heavily weighted socks (ie: a baseball in a sock).


TRACKING KILLS:

  • Once you have been killed, your assassin must confirm that you were indeed the correct target. If they hit the wrong person, you're still alive.
  • Assuming no mistakes were made, you will tell your assassin who your target was, and this becomes their new target.
  • If you have successfully assassinated your target, YOU MUST FIND MADDY HENRY OR ISAAC TEUSCHER AND REPORT YOUR KILL. We will be at a table on the patio during lunch this week waiting for kills to be reported. You can continue playing if you haven’t found one of us to report your kill, but it is essential that you report the kill at some time so we can determine who is still in the game and notify you.
  • If there is a dispute about the validity of a kill (ex. target used 3 stops that day, target was in a safe zone, etc), you will go before The Assassin Committee (made up of Junior Student Council members) who will hear your case. Please come find us on the patio during lunch if there is a dispute. If you know you were killed fair and square, please don't try to argue with your assassin, and simply give them their next target to ensure that the game can wrap up in a week.

SAFE ZONES:

  • Bathrooms
  • Lincoln Ave
  • The Parking Lot [Q] (driveway along field is not a safe zone)
  • The MEW [K]

No one may be killed during class or if a student is in a classroom with a teacher. If a student is alone in a classroom, not in class, and without a teacher, then kills are allowed.




STOPS:

If you see an assassin approaching you with a spoon or sock, you may yell "STOP!". If it was indeed your assassin, they CANNOT attempt to kill you for the 45 minutes after you told them to stop. YOU ARE ALLOWED TWO STOPS PER DAY (unless you are eliminated and buy-back).


PRIZES:

In the event that you win the game, you can choose to receive ONE of the following two prizes (prom tickets are only for upperclassman):

  • Two fully refunded tickets to prom (up to $200 value!)
  • A gift card to the store or restaurant of your choice ($140 value)


Buy-Backs

After a fair amount of players have been eliminated there will be a chance for players who have been eliminated to buy back into the game. The players who buy back will NOT have stops. Buy-Backs cost $10 and the date is TBD.
